# Anti-Money Laundering (AML) Policy

***

### 1. Introduction

URBNED INC. ("URBNED", "we", "us", or "the Company") is a registered Money Services Business (MSB) with the Financial Crimes Enforcement Network (FinCEN) of the United States Department of the Treasury.

We are committed to the highest standards of Anti-Money Laundering (AML) and Counter-Terrorist Financing (CTF) compliance. This policy outlines our approach to detecting, preventing, and reporting money laundering, terrorist financing, and other financial crimes across all of our services.

This policy applies to all customers, users, agents, and partners who access or use URBNED's services.

***

### 2. Our Services

URBNED INC. provides the following financial services, all of which are subject to this AML Policy:

* **Cross-Border Remittances** — International money transfers for individuals and businesses
* **Cryptocurrency Conversions** — Crypto-to-fiat and fiat-to-crypto exchange services
* **Virtual IBAN & Bank Accounts** — Virtual account services provided through licensed banking partners

***

### 3. Regulatory Framework

URBNED INC. operates in compliance with all applicable anti-money laundering laws and regulations, including but not limited to:

* **Bank Secrecy Act (BSA)** — 31 U.S.C. §§ 5311–5336
* **USA PATRIOT Act of 2001** — Title III
* **FinCEN Regulations** — 31 C.F.R. Parts 1010 and 1022
* **OFAC Sanctions Programs** — Office of Foreign Assets Control
* **Financial Action Task Force (FATF) Recommendations**
* **Anti-Money Laundering Act of 2020 (AMLA)**

***

### 4. Know Your Customer (KYC)

URBNED INC. is required by law to verify the identity of all customers before providing services. We operate a robust Customer Identification Program (CIP) in line with 31 C.F.R. § 1022.210.

#### 4.1 What We Collect

When you register with URBNED, we will ask you to provide:

**For Individual Customers:**

* Full legal name
* Date of birth
* Residential address
* Government-issued photo ID (passport, national ID, or driver's license)
* Tax identification number (SSN for US persons, or foreign equivalent)
* Contact details (phone number and email address)

**For Business Customers:**

* Legal business name and any trade names
* Business registration documents
* Employer Identification Number (EIN) or foreign equivalent
* Registered business address
* Beneficial ownership information (all individuals owning 25% or more)
* Details of authorized representatives

**For Cryptocurrency Services:**

* All standard individual or business KYC requirements apply
* Source of cryptocurrency funds documentation
* Blockchain wallet address(es)

#### 4.2 Why We Collect This Information

We collect this information to:

* Comply with our legal obligations under the Bank Secrecy Act
* Verify your identity and protect you from fraud
* Prevent our services from being used for money laundering or terrorist financing
* Meet OFAC sanctions screening requirements

#### 4.3 Identity Verification

All identity documents are verified through secure electronic verification processes. We may use third-party identity verification providers to assist with this process. Your information is handled in accordance with our \[Privacy Policy].

***

### 5. Risk-Based Approach

URBNED INC. applies a risk-based approach to AML compliance. This means we assess the level of risk associated with each customer and transaction and apply proportionate due diligence measures.

#### 5.1 Customer Risk Levels

Customers are assigned a risk rating upon onboarding:

| Risk Level | Examples                                                                                                  |
| ---------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Low**    | Verified individuals with low transaction volumes                                                         |
| **Medium** | Business customers, regular cross-border transfers                                                        |
| **High**   | Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs), high-volume crypto transactions, customers in high-risk jurisdictions |

#### 5.2 Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD)

For higher-risk customers or transactions, we may request additional information, including:

* Source of funds documentation
* Source of wealth documentation
* Purpose of transaction
* Senior management review and approval

We reserve the right to decline transactions or terminate relationships where adequate due diligence cannot be completed.

***

### 6. Transaction Monitoring

URBNED INC. monitors all transactions on an ongoing basis to detect unusual or suspicious activity. Our monitoring program includes:

* Automated screening of all transactions
* Review of transaction patterns against customer profiles
* Blockchain analytics screening for all cryptocurrency transactions
* OFAC and sanctions list screening for all customers and transactions

#### 6.1 Reporting Thresholds

In accordance with US law:

* **Currency Transaction Reports (CTRs)** are filed for cash transactions exceeding **$10,000** in a single business day
* **Suspicious Activity Reports (SARs)** are filed for transactions of **$2,000 or more** that appear suspicious
* We monitor for **structuring** — the practice of breaking up transactions to avoid reporting thresholds, which is illegal

***

### 7. Sanctions Compliance

URBNED INC. screens all customers and transactions against:

* OFAC Specially Designated Nationals (SDN) List
* EU, UN, and UK sanctions lists
* FinCEN advisories and geographic targeting orders

We are **unable to provide services** to individuals, entities, or jurisdictions subject to applicable sanctions. If a match is identified, the transaction will be blocked and reported to the relevant authorities as required by law.

***

### 8. Prohibited Activities

URBNED INC. does not knowingly facilitate and will report:

* Money laundering in any form
* Terrorist financing
* Fraud, including identity fraud and payment fraud
* Tax evasion
* Sanctions evasion
* Human trafficking or other criminal activity
* Transactions involving proceeds of crime

***

### 9. Suspicious Activity

If we suspect that a transaction or customer activity may be related to money laundering, terrorist financing, or other criminal activity, we are **legally required** to:

* File a Suspicious Activity Report (SAR) with FinCEN
* **Not inform the customer** that a SAR has been filed (this is called "tipping off" and is prohibited by law)
* Cooperate fully with law enforcement and regulatory authorities

***

### 10. Recordkeeping

We retain all customer and transaction records for a minimum of **five (5) years** in accordance with BSA requirements. Records are securely stored and accessible to regulatory authorities upon lawful request.

***

### 11. Staff Training

All URBNED INC. employees and agents who interact with customers or process transactions receive regular AML training. Our training program covers:

* BSA and regulatory requirements
* Customer identification procedures
* How to recognize and report suspicious activity
* OFAC sanctions compliance
* Cryptocurrency-specific AML risks
